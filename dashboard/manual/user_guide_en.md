# 📖 User Guide (How to Use)

> **App Goal:** To track and visualize copyright and rights infringement lawsuits arising from AI companies training models using unauthorized data, providing real-time geographical and relational insights.

This document guides you through the detailed features and premium utilization methods of the **AI Litigation Dashboard**.

---

### 1. 🚀 Data Loading & Visualization (Initial Setup)
*   **Dataset Selection**: Select a specific dataset from the `CSV Select` dropdown in the header to analyze a particular point in time.
*   **Refresh Visualization**: Before manually adjusting filters, click the `Update Map` button to synchronize the map and statistics with the latest data.

---

### 2. 🗺 Interactive Map Navigation
*   **Global Toggle**: Instantly switch between **USA (US)** and **Global (WORLD)** maps via the `REGION` menu in the header.
*   **High-Precision Zoom (Marquee Zoom)**:
    - **Shift + Drag**: Draw a rectangle over a specific area (e.g., Korea, Japan) to zoom in precisely to that region.
    - **Mouse Wheel**: Smoothly zoom in and out centered on your cursor position.
*   **Smart Tooltips**: Hover over any region to immediately see its name and the number of active lawsuits filtered in real-time.

---

### 3. 🎯 Advanced Multi-dimensional Filtering
*   **Defendant Filter**: Select a specific company (e.g., OpenAI, Meta, Google) to focus on litigation involving that entity.
*   **Theme Filter**: Filter by the data type involved in the lawsuit (e.g., Music, Visual Art, Code, Book) to identify industry-specific trends.
*   **Multi-Status Filter**: Select multiple progress statuses from the status dropdown to reflect them on the heatmap.
*   **Real-time Response**: All filters immediately update the map intensity and statistical figures.

---

### 4. 📈 Statistics & Trend Analysis
*   **Litigation Trend**: View monthly and yearly litigation frequency via the 'Litigation Trend' chart at the bottom of the **[STATISTICS]** menu.
*   **Comprehensive Stats**: Provides six core charts including claims, defendant market share, and court decisions.

---

### 5. 📱 Mobile User Guide
*   **Hamburger Menu**: On smartphones, use the **[MENU]** button at the top right to toggle the filter and control panel.
*   **Optimized View**: The control area dynamically adjusts to maximize the map view on smaller screens.

---

### 4. 🔍 High-Performance Case Search
*   **Integrated Search**: Enter keywords in the sidebar search box to simultaneously search across case titles, plaintiffs, defendants, and regional codes.
*   **Result Listing**: Matching lawsuits are listed as elegant cards; click any card to view detailed information immediately.

---

### 5. 📑 Case Details & Evidence
*   **Detail Modal**: Clicking a case card opens a premium popup containing plaintiff/defendant info, filing dates, court records, and case summaries.
*   **Official Document Access**: Use the `View Docket` button to instantly access original court documents on authoritative sites like **CourtListener**.

---

### 📥 6. Exporting Analytical Data
*   Click the `Export CSV` button in the regional summary popup to save the current statistical results and market share data as an Excel-compatible file.

---

### 7. 🔑 Key Status Definitions (Glossary)
The dashboard categorizes complex legal procedures into 6 clear stages:
- **Ready**: Lawsuit filed, but detailed documents are not yet public.
- **Started**: Official legal proceedings and discovery have commenced.
- **1st Instance**: Trial ongoing in a District Court.
- **2nd Instance**: Higher court review following a first-instance decision.
- **Decision**: Final judgment rendered by the presiding court.
- **Appeal**: Additional legal disputes ongoing following an appeal to a higher court.
- **Closed**: All proceedings finalized through judgment, settlement, or withdrawal.

---

### 8. 🕸️ Lineage Graph Analysis
An advanced visualization tool to understand the network between complex legal parties (Plaintiffs, Defendants, Datasets, Products).

*   **Access**: Click the **[Lineage]** button in the `RELATION` section of the header to open the analysis window.
*   **Interactive Nodes**:
    - **Plaintiff**: The party filing the lawsuit (Blue)
    - **Defendant**: The entity being sued (Red)
    - **Dataset**: Data used for training (Green)
    - **AI Product**: Related AI technology/product (Purple)
    - **Lawsuit**: Individual legal cases (Orange)
*   **Focus Mode**:
    - Select a target (Plaintiff/Defendant/Data) from the top-left search panel.
    - Selecting a target filters the view to show only the **direct litigation history and relationship network** for that entity.
*   **Graph Controls**:
    - **Zoom/Pan**: Use mouse wheel and drag to navigate freely.
    - **Details**: Click a node to view detailed info like case content, filing date, and reasons in the right panel.
